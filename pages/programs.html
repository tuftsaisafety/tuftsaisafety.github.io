Programs

Technical Fellowship

Applications for this fall have closed. Check back in January for the spring semester!

Our technical fellowship is an 8-week, in-person reading group that meets for 1.5hrs/wk to cover the foundations of frontier AI technology with topics like model interpretability, goal misgeneralization, reinforcement learning, oversight, and more.

The program is self-contained with no outside readings required and catered dinner/lunch is provided. The fellowship is open to undergraduates, graduate students, and postdocs looking to learn more about technical AI safety. Please direct questions to contact@tassa.dev.

Policy Fellowship

Applications for this fall have closed. Check back in January for the spring semester!

Our policy fellowship is an 8-week, in-person reading group that meets for 1.5hrs/wk to discuss governance and policy solutions for problems posed by transformative AI systems. We will read draft legislation, dissect governance frameworks, and develop a basic understanding of the social, political, and technical challenges of AI.

The program is self-contained with no outside readings required and catered dinner/lunch is provided. The fellowship is open to undergraduates, graduate students, and postdocs looking to learn more about AI policy. Please direct questions to contact@tassa.dev.

Capstone & Research Support

Interested in doing AI Safety Research at Tufts? Submit a Student Research Interest Form! Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis.

We offer capstone and research support - including compute - to members and those in our fellowships. Please direct questions to contact@tassa.dev.

Several Tufts labs can support AI Safety research, including but not limited to:

  • Tufts Institute for Artificial Intelligence (TIAI)
  • Human-Robot Interaction Lab (HRILab)
  • Goal Optimization using Learning and Decision-Making Lab (GOLD)
  • Multimodal Learning, Interaction, and Perception Lab (MuLIP)
  • Human Interaction Lab (HILab)
